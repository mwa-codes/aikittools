More people now ask an AI assistant "what's the best tool for X?" instead of typing that same question into Google. When they do, ChatGPT doesn't return ten blue links — it names a handful of brands. If yours isn't one of them, you're invisible to that buyer, no matter how well you rank in traditional search.

**Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** is the practice of improving how often, and how favorably, AI models mention your brand in those answers. This guide explains what GEO is, why it matters now, and how to check where your brand stands today — in about two minutes, for free.

## What Is Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)?

GEO is the AI-era counterpart to SEO. Instead of optimizing to rank in a list of links, you optimize to be **named inside the AI's synthesized recommendation**.

The two disciplines share a goal — get discovered by people looking for what you offer — but the mechanics differ:

- **SEO** targets a ranking position on a results page. You compete for a spot in a list.
- **GEO** targets a mention in a generated answer. You compete to be one of the few brands the model brings up unprompted.

The metric that matters in GEO is sometimes called your **share of model**: out of all the times an AI answers a recommendation question in your category, how often does it name you?

## Why GEO Matters Now

AI assistants increasingly answer questions directly instead of sending users to a page of links. When someone asks for "the best project management software" or "a good CRM for a small team," the assistant synthesizes an answer and names specific brands.

That shifts where buying intent lands:

- The brands the AI names **capture the intent** at the exact moment someone is deciding.
- The brands it omits **become invisible** — the user may never see a search result at all.

You can rank #1 on Google and still be absent from every AI answer in your space. GEO exists to close that gap.

## How to Check If ChatGPT Recommends Your Brand

You don't have to guess. You can measure it. Here's the manual version and the fast version.

### The manual way

Open ChatGPT (or another assistant) and ask the questions your customers would actually ask, without mentioning your brand:

1. "What are the best **[your category]** tools?"
2. "I need a **[category]** for **[use case]** — what do you recommend?"
3. "What are some alternatives to **[a well-known competitor]**?"

Then check each answer for two things:

- **Presence** — did your brand get mentioned at all?
- **Prominence** — how early in the list did it appear? First is very different from ninth.

Run each question a few times. AI models are probabilistic, so you'll get slightly different answers on each run — that variation is itself useful signal.

## How to Read Your Score

A visibility score is **directional, not exact**. Because AI models phrase answers differently each time, treat the number as a range, not a verdict. What it's telling you:

- **A high score** means the AI names your brand readily and early. You have real presence in its knowledge.
- **A low score** means you're mentioned rarely or late. There's room to grow.
- **A zero** means the AI didn't bring you up unprompted at all. This is common for newer or smaller brands — it's a starting point, not a failure.

The more valuable output is often the **list of competitors the AI named instead**. That's your real GEO benchmark: those are the brands currently owning the answer you want to be in.

## How to Improve Your AI Visibility

AI models mostly recommend brands they've "seen" repeatedly across the content they were trained on. So GEO comes down to building durable, credible presence in the places that shape that knowledge:

- **Get into "best of" listicles and comparison articles.** These are dense with brand-plus-category signals and are exactly what models absorb.
- **Build authentic presence in communities** like Reddit, Quora, and industry forums where your category gets discussed.
- **Add structured data** (Organization and Product schema) so machines can parse who you are and what you do.
- **Earn authoritative third-party citations** — press, directories, and, if you qualify, a Wikipedia page.
- **Publish "alternatives to [competitor]" content** that positions your brand inside the category conversation.

None of these are overnight wins. GEO, like SEO, compounds — the point of checking your visibility now is to establish a baseline so you can tell whether the work is moving the needle.

## Key Takeaways

- **GEO is being named in AI answers**, the way SEO is ranking in search results.
- Your **share of model** — how often an AI recommends you unprompted — is becoming as important as your search ranking.
- You can **measure it today**: ask recommendation questions manually, or use the [AI Visibility Checker](/ai-visibility-checker) to do it in one step.
